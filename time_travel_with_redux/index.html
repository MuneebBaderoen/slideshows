dealing with side effects

  • some users never kill apps
  • your app will be killed at every point in its lifecycle
  • we have to classify state by volatility
Some users keep apps running for days and weeks. These are usually the folks with enough memory on device for android/ios to take good care of them. When the os decides its time to deallocate your app, you might be in the middle of some important background task. You need to classify what state you're okay with losing, and what is critical for the app to function. Uncontrolled access to global state is a terrible idea, especially if that state only lives in memory
